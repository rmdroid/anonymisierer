Frequently asked questions

How does KI-Anonymizer work?

The app anonymizes text locally on your Mac. It is designed to clean up sensitive content before it is pasted into AI chatbots, email, documents, or other tools.

Does my text leave the device?

The anonymization step itself runs locally on the device. If you use this support page or the contact options listed here, the privacy notes on this page apply to those interactions.

Is the result always complete?

No. The app supports anonymization, but it does not replace human review. Before sharing, publishing, or using the output in a sensitive context, the result should always be checked.

Which platform is supported?

The app is designed for macOS. There may also be a browser-based web app, but it does not offer the same macOS workflow with global shortcuts and Apple Intelligence enhancement.

Important notes

  • The app does not replace legal, privacy, or professional assessment.
  • The result should always be checked manually before sending or publishing.
  • Apple NaturalLanguage and optional Apple Intelligence only support detection. They do not replace final human review.

App privacy

This privacy policy applies equally to the KI-Anonymisierer macOS app and the Chrome browser extension.

  • The app processes text locally on the device.
  • There is no cloud processing for the anonymization step.
  • The app uses no tracking, no analytics software, and no advertising.
  • There is no registration, no user account, and no subscription.
  • Text is not sent to third parties in order to perform anonymization.

Privacy

KI-Anonymizer is designed as a local macOS app that anonymizes sensitive text directly on the device. The anonymization step itself is performed without transmitting the text to external parties.

If you send a support request by email or through the contact form, the information you provide is used solely to handle your request.

The app itself uses no tracking, no advertising, and no user profiles. This support page itself uses no cookies and no user accounts. If you use external contact channels, the privacy policies of those providers also apply.
